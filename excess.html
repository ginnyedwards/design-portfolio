For this project I worked alongside the company ReSkinned, looking at how we can keeps items, materials and textiles in a circular economy. For this project I chose to work with and look into teddies and soft toys, something that I thought was interesting in a time where collecting teddies such as jelly cats has become extremely popular.

In order to keep teddies in circular economy we decided to explore how to extend the life cycle of soft toys through the standardisation of tags and labels. By looking at policy making and laws that tags have to make within the UK we were able to understand how to expand these rules in order to redesign a tag that we felt would aid in the extended lifecycle of soft toys and teddies. Our hope was that when people are better educated on what is in there teddy but also how to dispose of them properly, less teddies would end up in landfill.

The middle photo represents two iterations of our tag, one where all new materials have been used and one where recycled materials have been used.

The slideshow on the far right, shows other items that we made, one being our Eco Conscious logo, it is inspired by the fair trade logo known for certifying ethical and sustainable production across different industries. So we designed a symbol for responsibility toy manufacturing. Like fair trade, this mark assures consumers that a toy is made with sustainability in mind.

We designed extra tags that would be on toys, personalising and gamifying the tags encourages longevity and the incentive for people to keep the tags and the teddies. We also used the tags as ways to educate the users on the materials and lifecycle.

We also made a passport for the teddies, this passport is something that you get along side your teddy giving it a human like aspect. This passport can be given back to the shop with your teddy as sort of a buy back scheme and then sold on again with the same passport. By giving a teddy a passport to record adventures, we inspire longer-lasting love for your bear, helping reduce waste from discarded toys and supporting a more sustainable future.

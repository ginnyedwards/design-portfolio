This project in one that was based in escaping a chosen system. The system I chose was cannibalism, to do this I wanted to look into various ways that someone can live out their cannibalistic tendencies. My fascination for this topic came from the knowledge that cannibalism is legal in the UK and a number of other countries across the world. I researched and looked into different types of cannibalism such as auto cannibalism and pseudo cannibalism. So in this project I delve into the world of cannibalism, understanding and learning all of the laws used to police it. I wanted to Gove myself the best chance of legally becoming a cannibal. I learnt a lot about the topic, finding people who had successfully become cannibals and people who had fallen short and been arrested. To show all of the information I had learnt I made two iterations of a publication, one being more focused on imagery and the second (being the video playing on the right) one being more about the facts and information that I had gathered about being a legal cannibal in this world.

The photos of the jellied babies refer to part of the project where I looked into the efficiency of eating human meat or more specifically using human gelatine. With my project using a lot of jelly baby's to portray human and pseudo cannibalism I thought what if they made from human gelatine. To do this I figure out how gelatine is made by cows and converted it onto a baby. The average cow weighs 600 kg and will produce 73 kg of manufactured gelatine, this gelatine account for only 12.17% of the cows total mass. The average new born human baby weighs 3.4 kg and will produce 1.02 kg of manufactured gelatine. This accounts for 30% of the babies total mass. meaning in terms of gelatine produced per kilo of flesh the baby is far more efficient. From all of this I was able to work out how many jelly baby a human baby could make and this number came to 769. This jellied baby on the screen is one that I made, it is to represent all of the jelly babies made from one human baby with the weight of 769 jelly babies matching the weight of this baby. For this project I also made a pamphlet detailing how you could make your very own jellied baby.
